# 09 — Phase-2 Agri-Fintech Layer

This is how the "finance" interest is monetized — *after* the digital twin has
farm + FPO data and traction. Building it first would mean heavy regulation and
no proprietary data; building it second turns FarmTwin's data into a moat.

## The thesis
Once the twin runs across many farms, FarmTwin holds something lenders and
insurers cannot easily get: **verified, per-plot, time-series evidence** of water
use, crop health, stress events, and yield. That data de-risks three financial
products.

## Three products (in priority order)

### 1. Input / working-capital credit (lowest regulatory load)
- FarmTwin scores a farmer/FPO's repayment capacity from yield + practice data.
- Do **not** lend off our own balance sheet first. Act as a **DLG / origination +
  data partner** to an NBFC or bank, or route through the FPO. We earn a fee /
  revenue share, not interest, avoiding direct lending licences early.

### 2. Parametric / yield-linked crop insurance
- Use the twin's weather + soil-moisture + stress signals as the **parametric
  trigger** (e.g. consecutive stress days, rainfall deficit) for fast payouts.
- Partner with a licensed insurer / IRDAI-regulated entity; FarmTwin is the
  **risk-modelling + claims-trigger tech provider**, not the risk carrier.

### 3. Embedded finance in the FPO value chain
- Tie credit to verified procurement (FPO aggregation), de-risking the loan with
  an assured offtake — the cleanest structure for smallholders.

## Why sequence it second (not first)
- Regulatory: lending and insurance carry RBI/IRDAI obligations; partner-led,
  fee-based models keep us out of licence-heavy territory initially.
- Data: the credit/insurance edge *is* the twin's data — which only exists after
  Phase 1.
- Funding fit: KSUM funds the **tech product**; the fintech layer is a revenue
  expansion, fundable later via Scale-up / VC once revenue exists.

## Regulatory guardrails (note for later, with counsel)
- If we ever lend on own book -> NBFC registration; until then stay
  origination/DLG/LSP within RBI digital-lending guidelines.
- Insurance -> only as a licensed insurer's tech/analytics partner.
- Data -> explicit, separate farmer consent for any financial use (the pilot MoU
  in doc 08 deliberately does **not** grant this).

## Trigger to start Phase 2
- >= `<a few hundred>` farms with >= one full season of validated twin data.
- A signed NBFC/insurer partner LOI.
- Phase-1 SaaS revenue covering core costs.

## How it strengthens the overall story
It completes the founder's stated trifecta — **technology** (the twin),
**agriculture** (the pilot + FPOs), and **finance** (this layer) — as one
compounding data platform rather than three separate bets.
